The French Craft was delighted to propose, in partnership with the Mobilier national 's Laboratory for sustainable practices and the Big Bang Project agency, a day of exchanges around the issues of innovation and research in design and craft.

On Thursday, December 12, 2024, many of you joined us for the launch of the Mobilier national's Laboratory for sustainable practices.

Under the theme “National manufactures, sustainable manufactures”, this day of exchanges questioned the habitability of the world through 4 themes supported by expert speakers:

  • The first panel addressed materials and processes inspired by / based on nature, with Tony Jouanneau (Atelier Sumbiosis (external link)), Aurélie Mossé (Ensad Lab PSL Sacre (external link)) and Sandrine Rozier (external link) (Textile designer textile), and was hosted by Carole Petitjean (RDAI (external link)).
  • The second exchange, “Tomorrow, start-ups in the arts and crafts?" gathered Katie Cotellon (Saint-Gobain Research Paris (external link)), Bernard Leroy (Agglomération Seine-Eure (external link)), Thomas Paris (HEC - CNRS (external link)) and Éric Angiboust (Muzéo (external link)), and was hosted by Stefano Micelli (Université Ca' Foscari (external link) - Homo Faber Economy).
  • Third exchange, hosted by Marc Bayard (Mobilier national (external link)), addressed the redefinition of the contours of manual skills by digital technologies, with Julien Benayoun (Bold Design (external link)), Chloé Bensahel (external link) (Textile artist), Cyrile Deranlot (Daumet (external link)), Laurent Gaveau (Culture & Tech Consultant) et Anne Liberati (Consultant in color and trend forecasting).
  • The day ended with a debate on the necessary construction of collective imaginary worlds, bringing together Marc Aurel (external link) (Designer), Ramy Fischler (external link) (Designer), Aurélien Fouillet (Sociologist) and Élodie Ternaux (Hyloh (external link)), also hosted by Marc Bayard .
